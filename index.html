The materials that we will use can transport energy. Piezoelectric devices convert mechanical energy into electrical energy through the piezoelectric effect. This effect occurs when certain materials (such as quartz, ceramics, and some polymers) generate an electric charge in response to mechanical stress or vibrations.

1. Prepare Piezoelectric Material:
• Choose a piezoelectric material such as PZT powder or BaTiO₃ powder. If using fibers, cut them to the appropriate length.
• For powder, ensure it’s finely ground to ensure uniform mixing with the cement.
2. Mix Dry Components:
• In a concrete mixer, combine the Portland cement, aggregates (sand and gravel), and the piezoelectric powder. The amount of piezoelectric material typically ranges from 1% to 5% by weight of the total concrete mixture.
• If using fibers, distribute them evenly to avoid clumping.
3. Add Water and Mix:
• Gradually add water to the dry mixture while mixing to achieve a workable concrete consistency.
• Optionally, add a superplasticizer to improve the workability of the concrete, especially when using a high volume of piezoelectric material.
